I am a PhD candidate in the [NLP Group](https://minnesotanlp.github.io/) at the University of Minnesota, advised by [Dr. Dongyeop Kang](https://https://dykang.github.io/) and [Dr. Stephen J Guy](https://www-users.cse.umn.edu/~sjguy/).

## Research Interests
My research interests fall in the intersection of cognitive science and natural language processing. 

Some of my work aims to understand large language model behavior by decomposing it into functional cognitive components using approaches from psychology. (See our [project page](https://minnesotanlp.github.io/CogBench-web/) for more details.) For instance, we have found that LLMs have strong memory but weak attentional control, and we have found that LLMs do not make human-like pragmatic inferences in response to grammatical aspect, nor do they appear to impose coherent structures on narratives.

My other main research interest is in human cognitive dynamics and whether we can better align LLMs to how people plan, decompose complex tasks, and reason. To better understand human cognition in specific tasks, we have explored incorporating cognitive signals into annotations via [eye tracking](https://aclanthology.org/2023.conll-1.8/), [discrete reading behaviors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.21912), and [tracking longitudinal legal work across hierarchical subtasks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.18942).
